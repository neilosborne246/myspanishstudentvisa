Spain Student Visa TIE Card Application: Complete Step-by-Step Guide

The TIE card is your ID in Spain. You must apply within 30 days of arrival — here is exactly how to do it, what to bring, and what happens next.

Start Your Application ← Application Hub

The TIE (Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero) is the residence card issued to non-EU foreign nationals in Spain. As a student visa holder, getting your TIE within 30 days of arrival in Spain is not optional — it is a legal requirement. The TIE replaces your visa as your day-to-day identity document in Spain and is required for everything from opening a bank account to registering at the doctor's surgery. This guide walks you through the entire TIE application process step by step.

What Is the TIE and Why Do You Need It?

The Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero (TIE) is the official residence card for non-EU foreign nationals living in Spain. For student visa holders, it is the physical proof of your legal residence status. You need it for:

  • Proving your legal right to live in Spain to landlords, employers, banks, and public services.
  • Opening a Spanish bank account.
  • Registering with the public health system (tarjeta sanitaria).
  • Accessing student discounts and services at your institution.
  • Working legally in Spain (employers verify your work authorisation via the TIE).
  • Travelling within the Schengen Area as a legal resident.
  • Any future renewal (prorroga) application.

The Key Deadlines

The TIE application must be submitted within 30 days of your arrival in Spain. This is a legal requirement under Spanish immigration law. In practice:

  • Book your extranjería appointment (cita previa) within the FIRST 3–5 days of arrival — in cities like Madrid and Barcelona, appointments can have 2–3 week wait times.
  • Even if your appointment is more than 30 days from arrival, the booking confirmation (resguardo) serves as evidence you have initiated the process.
  • Do not wait until day 28 to book — appointment availability varies significantly and you risk missing the window.

How to Pay the Tasa 790 Código 012

The Tasa 790 código 012 is the government fee for the TIE application. Approximately €16. The payment process:

  • ('ol', ['Download the Modelo 790 Código 012 form from the Spanish Interior Ministry website.', 'Complete the form with your personal details — name, NIE (from your visa), address in Spain.', 'Take the completed form to any Spanish bank (Santander, BBVA, CaixaBank, etc.) and request payment of the tasa.', 'The bank processes the payment and stamps the receipt.', 'Bring the stamped original receipt to your TIE appointment.'])
  • Note: online payment of the Tasa 790 is also possible through the Sede Electrónica of the Interior Ministry, but many students find the bank payment simpler for their first application.

Booking the Extranjería Appointment

TIE appointments (cita previa para extranjería) are booked online through the Spanish Interior Ministry's appointment system:

  • Go to: sede.administracionespublicas.gob.es
  • Navigate to: Extranjería → Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero.
  • Select your province (not your nearest office — must be the office for your registered address province).
  • Select: Expedición de Tarjeta de Identidad de Extranjero — Régimen General.
  • Complete the online form with your passport and NIE details.
  • Appointments release at variable times — many students report that new slots appear early in the morning or after 6pm. Checking repeatedly over several days is usually necessary in major cities.
  • Once booked, you receive a confirmation email with appointment details — print or save this.

What to Do If Your Application Is Rejected

TIE applications are rarely refused for student visa holders who have all the correct documents. If you receive a deficiency notice (requerimiento) or refusal:

  • Read the notice carefully — it will specify what is missing or incorrect.
  • Gather the required additional documents.
  • Respond within the specified timeframe (typically 10 business days).
  • If refused outright, seek advice from an abogado de extranjería immediately — you have administrative appeal rights.
