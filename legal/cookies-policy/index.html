Cookie Policy

Last updated: 22 April 2026  ·  My Spanish Student Visa / Retail Consulting UK Ltd

This cookie policy explains what cookies are, which cookies we use on myspanishstudentvisa.com, and how you can manage your preferences. For broader information on how we handle your personal data, please read our Privacy Policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They allow the website to remember your actions and preferences over a period of time, so you don’t have to keep re-entering them when you return or browse between pages. Cookies can be first-party (set by us) or third-party (set by external services). They may be session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent cookies (stored for a set period of time).

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Ensure the website functions correctly (essential cookies)
  • Understand how visitors use our site so we can improve it (analytics cookies)
  • Measure the effectiveness of our advertising on platforms such as Meta (Facebook and Instagram) and Google Ads (marketing cookies)
  • Remember your cookie preferences (preference cookies)

4. Cookies We Do Not Use

My Spanish Student Visa does not use:

  • Cookies set by embedded third-party social media widgets
  • Cookies used for personalised retargeting beyond the Meta Pixel and Google Ads cookies disclosed above

5. Your Cookie Choices

When you first visit myspanishstudentvisa.com, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner where you can choose to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site, or by using your browser settings.

Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies through their settings menus. Note that blocking all cookies may affect the functionality of some features of this website, including the secure client dashboard.

You can also use the following tools to manage specific third-party cookies:

  • Opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Manage Google ad personalisation settings via Google Ads Settings
  • Manage your Meta ad preferences via Meta Ad Preferences

Browser-specific instructions for managing cookies:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

We may update this cookie policy from time to time to reflect changes in the cookies we use or in applicable law. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
