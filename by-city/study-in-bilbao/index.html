Studying in Bilbao on a Spain Student Visa

Bilbao is the capital of the Basque Country — a dynamic, forward-thinking city with a prestigious university, strong job market, and a different cultural atmosphere from the rest of Spain.

After Arrival

What to Do When You Arrive in Bilbao

All students arriving in Spain must complete empadronamiento and the TIE card process within 30 days. Here is what that looks like specifically in Bilbao.

Empadronamiento in Bilbao

  • Register at your local town hall (ayuntamiento)
  • Most require a cita previa (prior appointment) — book online
  • Bring: passport with student visa, proof of address (rental contract or student residence booking)
  • Certificate issued same day or within a few days
  • You need this certificate for your TIE appointment

TIE Card in Bilbao

  • Book through the CITA PREVIA system (sede.administracionespublicas.gob.es)
  • Select your province, then Extrajería, then the TIE appointment type
  • Bring: passport, empadronamiento certificate, enrolment letter, bank statements, health insurance, Tasa 790-012 receipt (~€16), 1 passport photo
  • Physical TIE card issued 4–8 weeks after appointment

It offers degree programmes at undergraduate, master's, and doctoral level, with some programmes available in English. Deusto University (Universidad de Deusto), a private Jesuit institution, also has a campus in Bilbao and is well regarded for business and law programmes. Both institutions receive international students and have experience supporting the Spanish student visa process.

After arriving in Bilbao, register your address at the Bilbao Ayuntamiento (empadronamiento) — this certificate is required for your TIE card appointment. Book your TIE appointment at the Oficina de Extranjería in Bilbao via sede.gob.es. Appointments are generally more available in Bilbao than in Madrid or Barcelona. Bring your passport, visa, empadronamiento certificate, enrolment letter, Tasa 790-052 receipt (approximately €16), health insurance proof, and a passport photo. The physical TIE card is issued approximately 2–4 weeks after your appointment.
Bilbao has a temperate oceanic climate — mild temperatures year-round but with significant rainfall, particularly in autumn and winter. Summers are pleasant (18–25°C) but shorter than in southern Spain. Students from warmer climates (Australia, South Africa, South America) often find Bilbao's green, rainy character a significant change from the Mediterranean destinations. This is not a negative — it reflects a genuinely different Spain that many students find more culturally interesting — but it is worth factoring into your choice of city.
