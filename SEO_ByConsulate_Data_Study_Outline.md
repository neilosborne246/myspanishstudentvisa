# Linkable Asset Outline — "Spain Student Visa Processing Times by Consulate"

Your single best link-earning opportunity. No competitor (Lexidy, SpainGuru,
Linguaschools) owns consulate-level processing data. You have 12 `/by-consulate/`
pages already built and — critically — your own application pipeline as a data
source nobody else can replicate.

## Why this wins links
- **Original data** = the #1 driver of editorial backlinks. Journalists,
  university international offices, and student bloggers cite numbers, not opinion.
- **Evergreen + updatable** = re-publish quarterly, earn fresh coverage each time.
- **Distribution-ready audiences**: university study-abroad offices, r/GoingToSpain,
  expat forums, study-abroad newsletters.

## The asset: a flagship pillar page
**URL:** `/by-consulate/processing-times/` (or upgrade `/by-consulate/` index)
**Title:** "Spain Student Visa Processing Times by Consulate (2026 Data)"

### Data to publish (per consulate)
| Field | Source |
|---|---|
| Median days, application → decision | Your own anonymised case data |
| Fastest / slowest observed | Your data |
| Appointment wait (days to first slot) | Your data + periodic manual checks |
| Approval rate (if sample is large enough) | Your data — *legal/PII review first* |
| Peak-season delay flag | Your data |
| Consulate-specific document quirks | Your case workers' knowledge |

### Page structure
1. **Headline finding** ("London & Dublin fastest; X slowest — full 2026 data").
2. **Sortable summary table** — all consulates, the money shot people screenshot.
3. **Methodology box** — sample size, date range, "based on N applications we
   handled." Transparency = citeability + trust.
4. **Per-consulate breakdown** — links down into each existing `/by-consulate/` page
   (turns the asset into an internal-linking hub for the cluster).
5. **"How to get the fastest decision" tips** — captures informational intent.
6. **Last-updated date + schema** (`Dataset` + `dateModified`).

## Hard guardrails (do before publishing)
- **PII / legal review with Platinum Legal Spain.** Aggregate only; never publish
  anything traceable to an individual applicant. Suppress any consulate cell where
  the sample is too small to anonymise (state "insufficient data" — don't guess).
- **No silent gaps.** If a consulate has thin data, say so. Credibility is the asset.

## Outreach (after publish)
1. **University international offices** (Madrid/Barcelona/Valencia + home-country
   unis sending students to Spain) — pitch as a free student resource. `.edu`-adjacent
   links are gold.
2. **Study-abroad & expat publications/newsletters** — pitch the headline stat.
3. **Reddit r/GoingToSpain, r/spain, expat Facebook groups** — share the table
   (value-first, not spammy).
4. **Digital PR**: tie the next refresh to a news hook (e.g. the RD 1155/2024 rule
   changes) for journalist pickup.

## Effort & cadence
- Build: ~2–4 weeks (data pull + legal review + page build).
- Refresh: quarterly — each refresh is a new outreach moment.
