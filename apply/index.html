Before You Apply

What you need before starting your Spain student visa application

You do not need everything ready on day one. Our free eligibility check confirms exactly where you stand. But here is what the Spain student visa application typically requires so you can start gathering documents early.

  • 📄
    Acceptance letter from a Spanish institution An official letter of admission from an accredited Spanish university, language school, vocational college, or research centre. The institution must be recognised by the Spanish Ministry of Education.
  • 🛂
    Valid passport Your passport must be valid for the full duration of your intended stay in Spain, plus a recommended buffer of at least six months beyond your planned departure date.
  • 💰
    Proof of sufficient funds Bank statements demonstrating you can cover your living costs in Spain for the full duration of your course. Consulates typically look for the equivalent of €600–€800 per month, calculated against Spain’s IPREM index. We tell you the exact figure.
  • 🏥
    Private health insurance A full-coverage policy with no co-payments and no deductibles, valid in Spain for the duration of your stay. We connect you with compliant student policies through our partner network at spanish-healthinsurance.com, accepted by all Spanish consulates.
  • 👮
    Criminal record certificate A police background certificate from every country you have lived in during the last five years, apostilled and accompanied by an official sworn translation into Spanish. We guide you through this step-by-step.
  • 📍
    Proof of accommodation in Spain Evidence of where you will live during your studies — a signed tenancy agreement, a university hall of residence confirmation, or a letter from the person you are staying with.

Eligibility

Six conditions to qualify for the Spain student visa

You must meet all six. We confirm your eligibility as the first step — at no cost — before you pay anything.

01

Non-EU nationality

The student visa is for non-EU/EEA passport holders. EU citizens can enrol at any Spanish institution without a visa.

02

Accepted to an accredited institution

An offer letter from a university, language school, or training centre recognised by Spain’s Ministry of Education.

03

Course longer than 90 days

The student visa is required only for studies exceeding 90 days. Shorter programmes are covered by a Schengen visa.

04

Sufficient funds

Bank statements showing enough to cover living costs for the full course duration — typically €600–800/month.

05

Private health insurance

A Spanish private health policy with no co-payments and no deductibles, valid for the full duration of your stay.

06

Clean criminal record

Police background certificates from every country you’ve lived in over the last 5 years, apostilled and translated.

Frequently Asked

Spain student visa application — questions we hear every day

How do I apply for a Spain student visa?

Start by creating an account on our 24/7 online dashboard at dashboard.platinumlegalspain.com/student-visa. A qualified immigration lawyer reviews your eligibility, prepares all your forms in Spanish, and submits your full application to the correct Spanish consulate in your home country. You pay €300 to begin and €499 only once your visa is approved.

Can I apply for a Spain student visa online?

Yes — the entire preparation process is managed online through your secure client dashboard. You upload documents, communicate with your case manager, and track progress from anywhere. The final application is submitted to the consulate on your behalf by our legal team. You will need to attend the consulate in person once to collect your visa after approval.

What do I need before I can apply?

Before applying you will need: an acceptance letter from an accredited Spanish institution, a valid passport, bank statements showing sufficient funds (typically €600–800/month), private health insurance valid in Spain with no co-payments, and a clean criminal record certificate (apostilled and translated). Our free eligibility check confirms exactly what applies to your situation.

How long does the Spain student visa application take?

Document preparation with our team typically takes 2 to 4 weeks depending on how quickly you gather your documents. Consulate processing after submission takes a further 4 to 8 weeks. We recommend starting at least 3 months before your course start date to allow adequate time at every stage.

Can I apply for a Spain student visa from inside Spain?

No — the Spain student visa (visado de estudios) must be applied for at a Spanish consulate in your home country before you travel to Spain. You cannot switch from a tourist visa to a student visa while in Spain. If you are already in Spain and enrolled in a course, contact us to discuss your specific options.

Do I need to attend a consulate appointment?

Yes — most Spanish consulates require an in-person appointment to submit your application or to collect your approved visa. We tell you exactly what your specific consulate requires and help you prepare for your appointment. The appointment is in your home country — not in Spain.

What nationalities can apply through your service?

We specialise in applications from the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Ireland, South Africa, and New Zealand. We know the specific requirements for every consulate in these countries. Applicants from other non-EU countries are also welcome — contact us to confirm eligibility and consulate requirements for your country.
